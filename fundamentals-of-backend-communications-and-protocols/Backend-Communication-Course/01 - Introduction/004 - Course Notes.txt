# 004 - Course Notes

**Section:** 01 - Introduction
**Duration:** 4min

---

## 1. Summary

In this lesson, the instructor adds an important note after receiving feedback from students. Some beginner students felt lost when the course mentioned protocols such as HTTP, TCP, and UDP early in the course.

The instructor explains that this is expected and completely normal. In the beginning sections, these protocols may be mentioned before they are fully explained. However, later sections of the course will cover each protocol in detail.

The instructor compares this learning process to learning a language. At first, learners may hear words they do not fully understand, but over time, repeated exposure helps the ideas become clearer.

The main advice is to keep going, absorb the information, and revisit earlier sections later. Concepts that seem confusing at first will make more sense after learning the fundamentals in later sections.

---

## 2. Key Concepts

### Beginner Confusion Is Normal

Students who are new to software engineering or backend engineering may feel confused when they hear terms like:

* HTTP
* TCP
* UDP
* Protocols
* Request / Response
* Long Polling
* Synchronous Workload
* Asynchronous Workload

The instructor reassures students that they do not need to fully understand everything immediately.

---

### Progressive Learning

The course introduces some concepts early, then explains them more deeply later.

This means learners may first encounter a term at a high level, then understand it better when the course reaches the dedicated protocol sections.

---

### Learning by Exposure

The instructor compares backend learning to language learning. When children learn a language, they hear words before fully understanding them. Over time, repeated exposure helps them understand naturally.

The same idea applies to backend engineering:

```text id="n0nvwp"
First exposure → Partial understanding → Deeper explanation → Rewatching → Stronger understanding
```

---

### Rewatching and Relearning

Students are encouraged to go through the course more than once. After learning the later sections, going back to earlier lessons can make the material clearer.

---

### Opening the Black Box

One of the goals of the course is to help students stop being afraid of backend systems. Instead of treating the backend as a mysterious black box, learners should open it, inspect it, and understand its internal parts.

---

### First Principles

The instructor emphasizes that many complex backend systems are built from basic fundamentals. Once students understand these first principles, backend systems become less intimidating.

---

## 3. Important Notes

* It is okay to feel lost at the beginning.
* Beginner students may struggle more because the course is labeled as intermediate.
* Early sections may mention concepts that are explained in detail later.
* Students should try to absorb the information even if they do not fully understand it immediately.
* Rewatching earlier sections after studying later sections can greatly improve understanding.
* The course is challenging for beginners, but it is still possible to complete.
* The goal is not just to memorize terms, but to understand what happens behind the scenes.
* Backend engineering becomes easier when learners understand the basic principles behind the abstractions.
* The course does not cover everything in backend engineering, but it provides a strong foundation.

---

## 4. Examples

### Example 1: Hearing HTTP Before Understanding It

At the beginning of the course, the instructor may explain request/response using HTTP.

A beginner may think:

```text id="1f7d49"
I do not know what HTTP is yet.
```

That is fine because HTTP will be explained in detail later.

---

### Example 2: Hearing TCP or UDP Early

The course may mention TCP or UDP while explaining backend communication.

At first, these may feel like confusing networking terms. Later, the course will explain:

* What TCP is
* What UDP is
* How they work
* Why backend engineers need to understand them

---

### Example 3: Rewatching Improves Understanding

A student may not fully understand the first section during the first watch.

After completing the protocol section, the student can go back to the earlier lessons and think:

```text id="1n4fql"
Now I understand what this means.
```

This is the learning process the instructor recommends.

---

### Example 4: Applying the Course at Work

After learning the fundamentals, a backend engineer may look at production code and say:

```text id="o1cdjl"
Now I understand what this code is doing behind the scenes.
```

This is one of the main goals of the course: to make engineers more confident and less dependent on black-box thinking.
